Vivir al amor de la vida.

A paradigm consists of a community’s ideals, observations, and practices. It contributes to a collective sense of reality and undergirds how the community organizes itself. A paradigm of interdependence—what Kriti Sharma calls radical contingency, Fritjof Capra calls the systems view of life, and I playfully rename living al amor de la vida— is coalescing in the global community. Below, I offer a translation of my phrase and a colloquial bibliography, outlining this cosmopolitan sense of connectivity.

Vivir al amor de la vida is a slang Spanish neologism based on two idioms.

1. Estar al amor de la lumbre “to be at the love of the flame” means to be close to the fire—typically a hearth or a bonfire.

2.Ir al amor del agua “to go to the love of the water” means to go with the current, the flow.

3. What might vivir al amor de la vida mean? To “live to the love of the life”?

I suggest that “al amor de” can indicate a copacetic relationship with the generous, perhaps wild nature of something, something such as fire, water, or life. Happily warm. Not fighting. In harmony. Moreover, since we humans are ~60% water, utilize a poetic kind of fire—oxygen-based metabolism that resembles combustion—within our cells, and are very much alive; in all three of these cases, the relationship in “al amor de” includes being another form of that generous wild.

Bibliography

The influences are listed in loose chronological order of their primary innovation. An asterix (*) indicates a major shift from an oral to a written tradition. These teachings their teachers hail from all over the Earth; they often converge with or influence each other. For now, I list the teachers from whom I have learned the most and omit many.

Organisms and earth formations are family members to care for and give care in return. Kincentricity; indigenous ecology*; Robin Wall Kimmerer, Enrique Salmón.

The universe is in continual flux and humans can live in harmony with it. The Tao and Wu-wei; Taoism; Lao Tzu.

A cultivated feeling of caring love toward all sentient beings that leads to devotion to their/our wellbeing and alleviation from suffering. Metta and bodhicitta; Buddhism*; The Buddha, The Fourteenth Dalai Lama, Thich Nhat Hahn.

Vast, loving forgiveness. This kind of grace seems, at least to me, needed sometimes. Mercy; Christianity*; Christ.

Living organisms are all kin related; they often enter into mutually caring relationships with one another; land masses are materially dynamic and teem with living organisms; and the metabolic processes of living organisms can affect the life-sustaining character of the local and global environments. Evolution, symbiosis, ecology, Gaia; western science; Charles Darwin, Lynn Margulis, James Lovelock.

The “births and deaths” of stars generate materials and habitable conditions for life; the study of seemingly distant, abiotic systems is also the study of life. Cosmology; astrophysics and big history; David Christian.

An innate affinity for living beings. Biophilia; western science; E.O. Wilson.

All living beings have merit and actions should be taken for their benefit. Deep ecology; western spirituality and science; Arnae Nass.

Sensually vibrant, equitable, caring relationships might pervade society, politics and economy. The erotic; feminism; Audrey Lorde.

Syntheses of the above. Systems thinking, The Work that Reconnects; Fritjof Capra, Joanna Macy.
